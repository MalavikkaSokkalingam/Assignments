Kiran Bedi

The First Woman IPS officer

Kiran Bedi 24th Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry.

Here's a time line of Kiran Bedi's life:

  • 1966 -Kiran Bedi was crowned the national junior tennis champion as a teenager. She won several titles at various national and state-level championships during this timeperiod.
  • 1972 - Kiran Bedi (born 9 June 1949) is an Indian social activist, former-tennis player who became the first woman in India to join the officer ranks the Indian Police Service (IPS).
  • 1979 - She started her career as an Assistant Superintendent of Police (ASP) in the Chanakyapuri area of Delhi, and won the President's Police Medal.
  • 1982 - Subsequently, as a traffic police officer, she oversaw traffic arrangements for the Asian Games in Delhi.
  • 1983 - The Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting 1983 in Goa. As Deputy Commissioner of Police of North Delhi, she launched a campaign against drug abuse, which evolved into the Navjyoti India Foundation.
  • 1993(May) - Kiran Bedi was posted to the Delhi Prisons as Inspector General (IG).Bedi decided to turn Tihar into a model prison. She introduced several reforms. She arranged separate barracks for the hardened criminals, who had been using their time in prison to recruit gang members, sell contraband and extort money.
  • 1994 -She introduced several reforms at Tihar Jail, which won her the Ramon Magsaysay Award.Bedi set up India Vision Foundation (IVF). IVF works in fields of police reforms, prison reforms, women empowerment and rural and community development.
  • 2003 - Kiran Bedi became the first Indian and first woman to be appointed head of the United Nations Police and Police Advisor in the United Nations Department of Peace Operations.
  • 2007 - She remained in service for 35 years before taking voluntary retirement in 2007 as Director General, Bureau of Police Research and Development.She resigned in 2007, to focus on social activism and writing. She runs the India Vision Foundation.
  • 2008 - Kiran Bedi hosted the reality TV show Aap Ki Kachehri on STAR Plus. In this court show, Bedi resolved everyday conflicts in a simulated courtroom. She launched the website to help people whose complaints are not accepted by the local police.
  • 2009 - Kiran Bedi was invited as the keynote speaker at a conference arranged by Aviation Industry Employees Guild.She is a social commentator and trainer and frequently speaks on various social issues like education, domestic violence & others.
  • 2011 - Kiran Bedi accepted the invitation, and the two had allied with other activists, including Anna Hazare, to form India Against Corruption (IAC) group. Their campaign evolved into the 2011 Indian anti-corruption movement.She was one of the key leaders of the 2011 Indian anti-corruption movement.
  • 2015 - Kiran Bedi joined the right-wing Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in January 2015. She unsuccessfully contested the 2015 Delhi Assembly election as the party's Chief Ministerial candidate.
  • 2016(May) - Earlier, on 22 May 2016, Bedi was appointed as the Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry.Kiran Bedi was the 24th Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry from 28 May 2016 to 16 February 2021.
  • 2016(May) - Kiran Bedi took oath as the Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry on 29 May 2016, and broke convention by addressing the gathering at the oath ceremony. She said she was there "on a mission to make the Union Territory of Puducherry a Prosperous Puducherry" and also gave "the TEA (Trust, Empowerment, Accountability) mantra to officers to work towards this mission."
  • 2019 - In 2019, as she celebrated her 70th birthday, she began 'Mission Green Puducherry' by planting saplings along the Kanagan lake in Puducherry. Since then, many students and volunteers have taken this forward by organizing tree planting drives.
  • 2019 - Kiran Bedi has introduced several best practices in Puducherry that are akin to management lessons that were outlined by her at the 50th Governors Conference in Delhi. From ensuring financial prudence, to bringing in community support, to having an open house, each of these practices have helped in massive development of Puducherry.
  • 2021(February) - She resigned as the lieutenant governor of Puducherry on 16 February 2021. The Governor of Telangana, Tamilisai Soundararajan was given additional charge of the Union territory.
